# Human Resources — Strategy & Task List

## Goal: Build capacity to scale beyond solo delivery without burning out

---

### Solo Founder Stage (Now)

- [ ] Define your personal working hours and protect them — burnout kills solo businesses
- [ ] Set a weekly "CEO hour" to work ON the business (strategy, marketing) not just IN it (client work)
- [ ] Track your time weekly: billable vs. non-billable — aim for 60%+ billable when fully ramped
- [ ] Identify your top 3 strengths and the 3 tasks you should eventually hand off first

### Freelancer & Contractor Network

- [ ] Identify 2–3 trusted freelancers you could subcontract to when capacity is full (designers, developers, copywriters)
- [ ] Research fair market rates for subcontractors in Melbourne (Upwork, Freelancer.com, local networks)
- [ ] Create a simple subcontractor agreement template covering: scope, rate, IP ownership, confidentiality
- [ ] Test one subcontractor on a low-risk internal task before putting them on a client project

### Culture & Values

- [ ] Write down 3–5 values that define how Kloudbiz works and treats clients (e.g. "fast response", "honest scoping", "quality over quantity")
- [ ] Use these values as hiring criteria when you bring on your first contractor or employee
- [ ] Reflect on these values quarterly — are day-to-day decisions consistent with them?

### First Hire Planning (6–12 months)

- [ ] Define the first role you'd hire for (e.g. part-time admin, junior developer, social media VA)
- [ ] Estimate the revenue level at which hiring makes financial sense (general rule: revenue > 3x their cost)
- [ ] Research Australian Fair Work obligations for employees vs. contractors
- [ ] Write a job description template for your first role before you need it

### Performance & Development

- [ ] Do a monthly self-review: what went well, what didn't, what to improve next month
- [ ] Invest in one skill or course per quarter relevant to your service offering (AI, design, SEO, sales)
- [ ] Track energy levels — note which tasks energise vs. drain you, and optimise your schedule accordingly
