# Marketing — Strategy & Task List

## Goal: Build consistent brand visibility and inbound leads

---

### Brand Foundation

- [ ] Finalise your brand tagline (one line that says who you help and how)
- [ ] Confirm brand colours, logo, and font are consistent across all touchpoints (website, WhatsApp DP, LinkedIn, email signature)
- [ ] Write your 3-sentence company bio for use across all platforms
- [ ] Create a professional email signature with name, role, phone, and kloudbiz.com link

### Website & SEO

- [ ] Add a portfolio/case studies section to kloudbiz.com (even 1–2 real projects)
- [ ] Add a testimonials section with real client quotes
- [ ] Submit kloudbiz.com to Google Search Console and verify ownership
- [ ] Target 3 local SEO keywords (e.g. "website design Melbourne small business")
- [ ] Add structured data (LocalBusiness schema) to index.html
- [ ] Ensure site loads in under 3 seconds on mobile (check via PageSpeed Insights)

### Social Media

- [ ] Set up or optimise LinkedIn Company Page for Kloudbiz
- [ ] Set up or optimise Instagram business profile
- [ ] Post once per week on LinkedIn (tips for small business owners, behind-the-scenes, client results)
- [ ] Post 2x per week on Instagram (portfolio screenshots, reels, before/after)
- [ ] Engage daily: comment on 3 posts from potential clients or local Melbourne business accounts

### Content Marketing

- [ ] Write your first "how-to" blog post or LinkedIn article (e.g. "5 reasons your Melbourne business needs a Google listing")
- [ ] Create a reusable WhatsApp poster template using poster.html for service promotions
- [ ] Produce one short-form video (30–60 sec reel) showing a website being built or a result achieved
- [ ] Build a simple email newsletter (use Mailchimp free tier) — even 1 send per month

### Paid Advertising

- [ ] Set a monthly ad budget (even $100 AUD is enough to test)
- [ ] Run one Google Local Services Ad or Google Search Ad targeting "website design Melbourne"
- [ ] Test one Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad targeting Melbourne business owners aged 30–55
- [ ] Track cost-per-click and cost-per-lead from each channel

### PR & Community

- [ ] Register Kloudbiz on local business directories (True Local, Yellow Pages, Yelp AU)
- [ ] Join and introduce yourself in 2 Melbourne small business Facebook groups
- [ ] Attend one local networking event or business chamber meetup (search Eventbrite Melbourne)
- [ ] Reach out to 1 local journalist or blogger covering Melbourne small business tech
