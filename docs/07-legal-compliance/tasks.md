# Legal & Compliance — Strategy & Task List

## Goal: Protect Kloudbiz and clients with clear agreements and compliant practices

---

### Business Registration

- [ ] Confirm ABN is registered and active at ABR.gov.au
- [ ] Decide on business structure: Sole Trader (simplest to start) vs. Pty Ltd (more protection, more admin)
- [ ] Register the business name "Kloudbiz" if trading under that name (ASIC Business Name Register)
- [ ] Consider professional indemnity insurance — especially important for digital/tech services

### Contracts & Agreements

- [ ] Write or source a standard Client Services Agreement covering: scope, payment terms, IP ownership, revisions, and liability cap
- [ ] Create a simple "Project Brief" template clients sign before work starts
- [ ] Define your revision policy clearly in every quote (e.g. "2 rounds of revisions included")
- [ ] Ensure every quote is followed by a written acceptance (email or WhatsApp confirmation is acceptable)
- [ ] Do NOT start work without a deposit and written agreement

### Website Legal Pages

- [ ] Add a Privacy Policy to kloudbiz.com (required under Australian Privacy Act if collecting personal data)
- [ ] Add Terms of Service to kloudbiz.com
- [ ] Ensure the contact form includes a privacy notice ("Your details are used only to respond to your enquiry")
- [ ] If running Google Ads or Meta Ads, ensure cookie consent is handled correctly

### Intellectual Property

- [ ] Clarify in your contract: client owns final deliverables, Kloudbiz retains right to show work in portfolio
- [ ] Do not use paid stock photos, fonts, or plugins in client work without a valid licence
- [ ] Keep a licence record for every third-party asset used in client projects

### Compliance

- [ ] Comply with Australian Spam Act: all marketing emails must include an unsubscribe option
- [ ] Comply with Australian Consumer Law: no misleading claims in your marketing copy
- [ ] If storing client data, ensure it is held securely and not shared with third parties without consent
- [ ] Review Privacy Policy annually or when data practices change
